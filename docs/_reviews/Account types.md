
That is a really solid foundation for organizing your finances. Grouping your accounts logically like this makes it much easier to track your cash flow, analyze your net worth, and keep your financial life uncluttered.

Let’s break down specific account types that fit perfectly into your current groups, and then look at a couple of additional categories you might want to add to catch anything that slips through the cracks.

### 1. Your Current Groups

**Employment** _Accounts tied directly to your job, benefits, or compensation._

- Direct Deposit / Payroll accounts (if tracked separately before hitting your bank)
    
- Health Savings Accounts (HSA)
    
- Flexible Spending Accounts (FSA)
    
- Employer-sponsored stock plans (Employee Stock Purchase Plans, Restricted Stock Units)
    
- _Note: 401(k)s or pensions often live here, though they can also be grouped under Investments._
    

**Business** _Accounts strictly isolated for an LLC, sole proprietorship, freelance work, or side hustle._

- Business Checking Accounts
    
- Business Savings Accounts
    
- Merchant Services / Payment Gateways (Stripe, PayPal Business, Square balances)
    
- Corporate/Business Credit Cards
    
- Petty Cash accounts
    

**Credit Cards** _Revolving, unsecured personal debt._

- Everyday Cash Back / Rewards Cards
    
- Travel / Premium Credit Cards
    
- Store / Retail Credit Cards (e.g., Target RedCard, Amazon Prime Visa)
    
- Balance Transfer / 0% APR promotional cards
    

**Investments** _Assets meant for long-term wealth building and market growth._

- Taxable Brokerage Accounts (Stocks, bonds, ETFs, mutual funds)
    
- Individual Retirement Accounts (Traditional IRA, Roth IRA)
    
- Robo-Advisor Accounts (e.g., Betterment, Wealthfront)
    
- Cryptocurrency Wallets / Exchange balances
    
- Education Savings Accounts (529 Plans, Coverdell ESAs)
    

**Savings** _Liquid or semi-liquid cash reserves meant for safety and short-to-medium-term goals._

- High-Yield Savings Accounts (HYSA)
    
- Traditional Bank Savings Accounts
    
- Certificates of Deposit (CDs)
    
- Money Market Accounts (MMAs)
    
- Sinking Funds (e.g., dedicated sub-accounts for "Vacation" or "Home Repairs")
    

### 2. Suggested Groups to Add

Looking at your list, you cover a lot of ground, but you might run into trouble categorizing everyday cash flow and non-credit-card debt. You might want to consider adding these two top-level groups:

**Everyday Banking / Checking** _The central hubs where your money flows in and out on a daily basis._

- Personal Checking Accounts
    
- Joint Checking Accounts
    
- Cash Management Accounts (often offered by brokerages as checking alternatives)
    

**Loans / Debt (Installment)** _Credit cards cover your revolving debt, but you'll likely want a separate category for fixed-term liabilities._

- Mortgages
    
- Auto Loans
    
- Student Loans
    
- Personal Loans
    
- Buy Now, Pay Later balances (Klarna, Affirm)
